Star Wars Facts

A long time ago, in a galaxy far, far away...

A New Hope's Original Title

The first film, released in 1977, was originally just titled "Star Wars." The subtitle "Episode IV – A New Hope" was only added in 1981, after the release of "The Empire Strikes Back," to help establish the film as part of a larger saga.

Yoda's Early Design

Before Frank Oz was hired to operate the puppet, George Lucas considered having a monkey in a Yoda costume for the role. Thankfully, that idea was scrapped in favor of the wise, green Jedi Master we all know and love.

The Wookiee's Real-Life Muse

George Lucas’s Alaskan Malamute, Indiana, was the inspiration for Chewbacca. The dog often sat in the passenger seat of his car, leading to the idea of a large, hairy co-pilot for Han Solo.

That Iconic TIE Fighter Sound

The distinctive sound of a TIE Fighter flying by was created by combining the sound of a car driving on a wet road with an elephant's trumpeting. This unique blend gave the ships a menacing and alien roar.

A Falcon's Original Title

The famous ship, the Millennium Falcon, was originally called the "Starkiller." This name was later repurposed for the main hero's original family name, Luke Starkiller, before it was changed to Skywalker.

The Running Gag Line

The phrase "I have a bad feeling about this" is a recurring line in every single Star Wars film. It's said by a different character each time, making it one of the most consistent running gags in movie history.
